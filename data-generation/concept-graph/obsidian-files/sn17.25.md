At Sāvatthī.
“Mendicants, there are ascetics and [[brahmin]]s who don’t truly understand the gratification, drawback, and escape when it comes to possessions, honor, and popularity.
I don’t deem them as true ascetics and [[brahmin]]s. Those venerables don’t realize the goal of life as an ascetic or [[brahmin]], and don’t live having realized it with their own insight.
There are ascetics and [[brahmin]]s who do truly understand the gratification, drawback, and escape when it comes to possessions, honor, and popularity.
I deem them as true ascetics and [[brahmin]]s. Those venerables realize the goal of life as an ascetic or [[brahmin]], and live having realized it with their own insight.”
